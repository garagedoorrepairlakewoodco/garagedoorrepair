Factors That Affect Garage Door Repair Costs

Several variables influence the final price you'll pay for garage door repairs in Lakewood. Understanding these helps you get accurate estimates.

Door Size and Weight

Single-car doors (8-9 feet wide) cost less to repair than double-car doors (16-18 feet). Heavier doors require stronger springs and components, increasing costs by 20-30%.

Type of Material

Steel doors have readily available parts. Wood doors may need custom components. Aluminum and specialty materials can cost more for replacement parts.

Age of Door

Doors over 15 years old may have discontinued parts, making repairs more expensive or impossible. Sometimes replacement becomes more cost-effective than repair.

Extent of Damage

Simple repairs like sensor adjustment cost less than major structural repairs. Severe damage from accidents or weather may require multiple component replacements.

Location and Accessibility

Lakewood's central location keeps costs reasonable. However, difficult access, steep driveways, or unusual door configurations may add labor time and cost.

Time of Service

Regular business hours (Monday-Friday, 8 AM-5 PM) have standard rates. Evening, weekend, and holiday service typically adds $100-150 to the total cost.

Parts Quality

Economy parts cost less upfront but may need replacement sooner. Premium parts cost 20-30% more but last longer and often come with better warranties.

When to Repair vs Replace Your Garage Door

Sometimes replacement makes more financial sense than repair. Here's how to decide:

Repair Makes Sense When:

  • Door is less than 15 years old
  • Only one or two components need replacement
  • Door structure is sound with no major damage
  • Repair cost is less than 50% of replacement cost
  • Door style matches your home and you're satisfied with appearance

Consider Replacement When:

  • Door is 20+ years old with frequent issues
  • Multiple components failing (springs, cables, panels, opener)
  • Severe structural damage from accidents or weather
  • Poor insulation causing energy loss
  • Outdated style affecting home value
  • Total repair costs exceed 50% of new door price
  • Parts are discontinued or hard to find

Quick Math: If repairs will cost $800 and a new door costs $1,500, replacement might be smarter. You get a modern, energy-efficient door with full warranty versus an old door with patched repairs.
